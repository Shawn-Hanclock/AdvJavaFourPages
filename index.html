Plants and Math

Some people like math, others may not. But how are plants related to math, and how does it connect to nature? (and maybe how is this math related to computer science) Have you ever thought about how mathematical patterns relate to the nature of the world? Math and mathematical models are ultimately created to understand our world, and as we will see, plants and nature are places where even math reaches.

The late Alan Turing, the famous mathematician and computer scientist, was particularly interested in the phenomenon, the connection of math and nature. He was curious to know how biology connects to mathematics, or in other words, how mathematics can predict nature. Alan Turing, before passing, made significant contributions in the field, creating a basis for how we understand biological development today.

An interesting thing to mention from Turing's work is his theories about pattern formation in nature. Turning Patterns. To keep it simple, what he found was that simple chemical reactions lead to the emergence of complex and structured patterns in biology. Alan Turing used math to understand how we grow fingers.
